Drywall Repair Resource Center

Learn how drywall becomes damaged, what repair methods are commonly used, how to recognize problems early, and how proper maintenance helps keep your walls looking their best.

What Causes Drywall Damage?

  • Furniture impacts
  • Door handle damage
  • Building settlement
  • Water leaks
  • Roof leaks
  • Plumbing failures
  • Humidity changes
  • Poor installation
  • Accidental holes

Signs That Drywall Needs Attention

Not every crack or hole requires immediate replacement, but drywall should be inspected when you notice movement, soft spots, bubbling paint, stains, loose tape, recurring cracks, or visible mold. Small cosmetic damage can often be repaired before it develops into a much larger project.

Typical Drywall Repairs

  • Hole patching
  • Crack repair
  • Texture matching
  • Corner bead replacement
  • Ceiling repairs
  • Joint tape replacement
  • Skim coating
  • Paint preparation

Preventative Maintenance Tips

Inspect walls several times each year, especially after plumbing leaks or heavy storms. Keep indoor humidity under control, repair roof or plumbing leaks promptly, and protect corners and high-traffic areas from repeated impacts. Routine maintenance helps extend the life of drywall and reduces the need for larger repairs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can drywall always be repaired?

Many types of drywall damage can be repaired successfully, but severely water-damaged or mold-contaminated drywall may need to be replaced.

How do I know if the damage is structural?

Wide, recurring, or stair-step cracks can indicate movement in the building and may require further evaluation before cosmetic repairs are made.

Should drywall always be painted after repair?

Most repairs require primer and paint to achieve a consistent appearance and protect the repaired area.
